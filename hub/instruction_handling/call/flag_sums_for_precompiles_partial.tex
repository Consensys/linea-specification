We provide some \textbf{partial peeking flag sums for precompiles} below.
We can't precisely predict the number of required rows at this point in time.
We will be setting \nonStackRows{} and associated peeking flag sums on the scenario row that marks the end of the first half of peeking flags for \inst{CALL}'s to precompiles.
This will be achieved in
section~(\ref{hub: instruction handling: call: precompiles: generalities}),
section~(\ref{hub: instruction handling: call: precompiles: flag sums and nsr I}) and
section~(\ref{hub: instruction handling: call: precompiles: flag sums and nsr II}).
\begin{description}
	\item[\underline{Precompile failure:}]
		\[
			\peekingSumPrcFailureFH
			\define
			\left[ \begin{array}{lcl}
				+ & \extendedPeekingSumForCalls                                 \\
				+ & \peekScenario _{i + \callPrcFailureSecondScenarioRowOffset} \\
			\end{array} \right]
		\]
	\item[\underline{Precompile success, current context will revert:}]
		\[
			\peekingSumPrcSuccessWillRevertFH
			\define
			\left[ \begin{array}{lcl}
				+ & \extendedPeekingSumForCalls                                            \\ \hline
				+ & \peekAccount  _{i + \callSecondCallerAccountRowOffset                } \\
				+ & \peekAccount  _{i + \callSecondCalleeAccountRowOffset                } \\
				+ & \peekAccount  _{i + \callSecondDelegateOrCalleeAccountRowOffset      } \\ \hline % TBD ... we may do this just for simplicity's sake
				+ & \peekScenario _{i + \callPrcSuccessWillRevertSecondScenarioRowOffset } \\
			\end{array} \right]
		\]
		\saNote{}
		For context,
		\begin{itemize}
			\item \atRevertTime{} account-row $n°(i + \callSecondCallerAccountRowOffset           )$ pertains to the \callerName{},
			\item \atRevertTime{} account-row $n°(i + \callSecondCalleeAccountRowOffset           )$ pertains to the \calleeName{},
			\item \atRevertTime{} account-row $n°(i + \callSecondDelegateOrCalleeAccountRowOffset )$ pertains to the \delegateName{} or \calleeName{},
		\end{itemize}
		With successful \textsc{prc}-calls that are rolled-back one must
		undo balance transfers both for the caller and the (precompile) recipient.
		Note that there are no ``warmth''-related changes to undo since precompile addresses are warm by default,
		though the constraints may implement undoing operations anyway (that amount to \texttt{Noop}'s).
		All of this happens \atRevertTime{}.

		\saNote{}
		Recall that the only way to trigger precompiles is by directly targeting them,
		by which we mean that the (trimmed) address argument popped off of the stack by the
		\inst{CALL}-type instruction must be that of a precompiled contract.
		Account delegation, as enabled by \cite{EIP-7702}, doesn't allow for
		calling \textsc{prc}'s through delegation.
	\item[\underline{Precompile success, current context won't revert:}]
		\[
			\peekingSumPrcSuccessWontRevertFH
			\define
			\left[ \begin{array}{lcl}
				+ & \extendedPeekingSumForCalls                                           \\
				+ & \peekScenario _{i + \callPrcSuccessWontRevertSecondScenarioRowOffset} \\
			\end{array} \right]
		\]
\end{description}
